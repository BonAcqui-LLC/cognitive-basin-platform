First Life Archive

Before the framework became a stack of documents, it was also an image intuition.

The First Life Archive collects the imagery, notes, and conceptual fragments around early branching, green-lightning forms, first visible contact, and the recurring idea that life writes itself into structure before it writes itself into language.

This lane is less about proof than orientation. It helps explain why branching, emergence, tension, and fragile first contact keep showing up as the emotional seed of the project.

For new readers, this archive gives emotional coherence to the rest of the stack. It shows that the project did not begin as a classifier or a descriptor list. It began with a repeated confrontation with forms that felt like they were trying to say something about process.

Publicly, this archive should function as a carefully framed image-and-note collection, not as cosmology or grand explanation. It is a record of the origin imagery behind the thesis.

Over time, this archive may connect the visual language of roots, scars, discharge paths, rivers, dendrites, and volcanic ash to the more formal language of MCVA, Natural Math, and Morphological Memory Theory.
