# Natural Math Integration

Natural Math is the forward or generative layer in the stack.

## Role

Natural Math is a constrained local-process simulation framework. It is useful for generating candidate morphologies under finite local rules, bounded sensing, dynamic limits, and local memory.

MCVA then operates as the inverse or readout layer, comparing observed morphology against baselines and candidate generated histories.

## Boundary Conditions

- Natural Math is not claimed as a universal theory.
- Local-update modeling is not new; it is adjacent to cellular automata, agent-based modeling, stigmergy, diffusion-limited aggregation, L-systems, graph growth, path planning, and dynamical systems.
- SENSE is a process state inside the generative model.
- HOLD is a decision or runtime state in the readout stack.

## Key Mechanism

The most important bridge phrase is:

**Memory becomes geometry.**

Local trails, blockages, reinforcement, depletion, and archive marks can alter future behavior and therefore the shape that ultimately appears.
