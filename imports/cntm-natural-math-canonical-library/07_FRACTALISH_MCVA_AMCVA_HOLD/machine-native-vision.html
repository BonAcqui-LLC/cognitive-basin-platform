Machine-Native Vision

Machine-native vision asks what happens when sensing stops trying to imitate the human eye first.

In this lane, the goal is not simply to turn sensor data into a picture for a person. The goal is to detect, preserve, and emit morphology-bearing signals directly: branch patterns, fracture topology, erosion fronts, vascular structures, spatial roughness, and other forms that may function as process evidence.

This page belongs to the future-facing perception side of the stack. MCVA is about readout. Natural Math is about generation. Machine-native vision is about what kind of sensing substrate might feed those layers well.

Infinitysight belongs here as a future perception layer. It is about perception, not proof. A machine-native system may become better at seeing morphology across sensors and scales, but that does not mean it automatically knows what the morphology means.

For contributors, this is a research lane rather than a shipping claim. It points toward sensing pipelines, robotics, inspection, multimodal morphology extraction, and machine-native tokenization without pretending those pieces are already solved.

The public release should keep this lane aspirational and research-first.
