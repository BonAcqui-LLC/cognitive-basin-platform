# MCVA / AMCVA: An Open Framework for Reading Process from Form

## Abstract

MCVA is an open research framework for measuring morphology-bearing structures as process evidence while preserving uncertainty through a first-class HOLD state. The framework combines descriptor extraction, ternary decision gating, provenance records, Natural Math generative simulations, and SERA efficiency metrics to compare observed morphology against candidate process histories. MCVA is not a medical diagnostic system, not a universal theory, and not a replacement for domain experts. It is a public research architecture for making morphology work more testable, comparable, and falsifiable.

## 1. Open Release Doctrine

This release is public-first. The goal is to establish a timestamped public record, make the ideas usable, invite criticism and contribution, and prevent the work from disappearing behind obscurity or closed gatekeeping.

The core framework is open. If this work helps you, support it, cite it, contribute back, sponsor development, or hire us to apply it.

## 2. MCVA, AMCVA, and HOLD

MCVA stands for **Morphological Computation Vocabulary Atlas**. It is the positive or readout layer: the framework for identifying when observed morphology contains enough structure to be measured, compared, and discussed as process evidence.

AMCVA is the guardrail or negative atlas. It covers absent, erased, hidden, misleading, blurred, contaminated, low-resolution, or otherwise non-diagnostic morphology.

HOLD is the unresolved evidentiary state. HOLD means the system should not force a binary answer when the evidence, segmentation, metadata, or baseline support is insufficient.

## 3. Morphological Memory Theory

The broader thesis is narrow and practical: some structures may preserve partial evidence about the process that produced them. This does not imply universal equivalence, perfect reversibility, or unique cause identification. It implies a research discipline:

1. preserve morphology-bearing structures,
2. measure them explicitly,
3. compare them to baselines and candidate generated histories,
4. preserve uncertainty rather than force overclaim.

## 4. Natural Math as the Generative Layer

Natural Math is the forward or generative layer of the stack. It is a constrained local-process simulation framework based on finite local updates, bounded sensing, dynamic limits, process states such as `EXTEND`, `SENSE`, and `RESTRICT`, and memory fields that can alter future local boundary conditions.

MCVA is the inverse or readout layer.

The bridge concept is:

**Memory becomes geometry.**

Local trails, erosion, reinforcement, depletion, blockage, and archive marks can shape later behavior and therefore the resulting morphology.

## 5. Observed vs Generated Morphology

The framework is strongest when observed morphology is compared against:

- domain baselines,
- AMCVA boundary cases,
- and candidate generated traces from constrained simulations.

This does not guarantee exact causal recovery. It does create a reproducible workflow for asking whether observed structure is more consistent with some process histories than others.

## 6. SERA

SERA measures workflow cost and decision quality, including:

- waste,
- retries,
- false commitments,
- HOLD rate,
- and valid committed output.

The point is to avoid judging the framework only by forced-commit accuracy.

## 7. Atlas Strategy

The atlas should be built through manifest-first publication. Every public example should carry source, rights, modality, domain, morphology tags, and AMCVA boundary notes.

If the rights to publish the underlying asset are unresolved, publish only a manifest entry.

## 8. First Application Lane: Roads and Public Works

The first application lane should be roads and public works. It is comparatively low-risk, visually obvious, commercially understandable, and public-data friendly.

Suggested framing:

**SERA for roads: morphology-based road quality assessment and maintenance prioritization.**

This is a proposed open protocol, not a certified standard.

## 9. Medical and Research Caution

Medical or biomedical morphology may be scientifically interesting, but it carries far higher risk. Current public release language should stay in research-only territory and avoid any suggestion of diagnosis, treatment recommendation, or regulatory certification.

## 10. Non-Claims

This project does not currently:

- diagnose disease,
- predict disasters,
- replace engineers, clinicians, or domain experts,
- prove a universal law,
- or certify regulatory decisions.

## 11. Citation Request

If this work helps you, please cite the repository and the whitepaper, and share improvements back when possible.

## 12. Conclusion

MCVA is best understood as an open framework for careful morphology work. It asks readers to preserve uncertainty, compare before claiming, and treat observed form as potential process evidence without pretending the evidence is stronger than it is.
