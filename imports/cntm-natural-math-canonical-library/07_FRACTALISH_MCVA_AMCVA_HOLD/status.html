Status

Stable enough to review. Not finished.

This page separates what is stable, what is actively evolving, what remains hypothesis, and what should not be claimed.

Stable

Fractalish, Natural Math, MCVA, AMCVA, HOLD, decision points as data points, recovery wake, Cognitive Basin, Activation Runtime, operator-sovereign runtime language, and not-consciousness simulation boundary posture.

Actively evolving

Geometry-state as native script, morphology inversion, quantified AMCVA, cross-domain comparison scoring, and the coupling between Natural Math generation and Fractalish readout.

Current proof targets

Recover hidden generating parameters from synthetic morphology better than chance; compare inferred morphology families against baselines; and preserve uncertainty honestly where no stable readout exists.

What remains hypothesis

  • That process memory can be recovered robustly enough to support cross-domain comparison beyond analogy.
  • That decision points can be parameterized and compared across sufficiently different physical and cognitive regimes without collapsing important differences.
  • That Cognitive Basin logging will yield a stable morphology vocabulary rather than just interesting internal telemetry.

What is not claimed

  • No claim of consciousness.
  • No claim of sentience.
  • No claim of artificial personhood.
  • No medical diagnostic claim.
  • No regulatory certification claim.
  • No proof of a universal law.
  • No claim that all morphology is readable.
  • No claim that fractals explain everything.
  • No claim that Natural Math models all domains.
  • No claim that AI basins prove human cognition.

Constructive review posture

Review is most useful when it names the exact claim, evidence state, file version, and counterexample needed to weaken or strengthen the work. Hostility to overclaim is welcome. Casual dismissal is not very informative.
