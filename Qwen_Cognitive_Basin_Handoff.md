I am providing a new canonical working thesis for the Fractalish / Cognitive Basin project:

**Latent Role Intelligence and Sparse Basin Realization: A Compute-Bounded Thesis for Fractalish / Cognitive Basin Architectures**

Read the attached thesis in full before responding. This document supersedes earlier descriptions wherever those descriptions treat ATAL, RIGOR, PERCEPT, affect, reasoning, memory, or action governance as single monolithic cognitive agents.

The central revision is:

- Human-scale nuance requires many purpose-built role intelligences.
- Those roles must not all remain continuously instantiated.
- Each role persists primarily as a compact **Role Kernel**.
- A full **Role Process** is reconstructed only when sparse, ternary, event-driven activation warrants it.
- After intervention, it writes a typed basin report, updates a compact **Role Residue**, and terminates.
- **HOLD** is an active subscription with release conditions, not continuous background reasoning.
- The basin is a shared event-sourced coordination field, not a transcript and not a majority-vote agent chat.
- The design target is code-dense but execution-sparse: many latent capacities, few realized processes at any moment.
